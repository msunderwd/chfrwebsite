1. General Operating Rules

The CH&FR General Operating Rules are incorporated here by reference.  Additional Rules specific to operations within the Frost River Sub are outlined in the subsections below

2. Special Instructions

2.1 Standard Clocks, Bulletin Books and Train Registers

The Standard Clock, Bulletin Book and Train Register are kept at the locations indicated in the subdivision-specific sections of the timetable. All clocks are kept at a 1:1 clock ratio, unless otherwise specified by the Train Master.

The Standard Clock is the JMRI Fast Clock running on the train room computer.

2.2 Train Register

All Track Warrants and Train Orders shall be recorded in the Train Register. Trains not listed in the Timetable shall not be permitted to operate outside of Yard Limits without a proper Track Warrant or Train Order.

Train and Work Crews are responsible for reviewing the Train Register prior to beginning work, and for recording their operations as necessary in the Train Register.

The Train Register is on the train room computer.

2.3 Standard Car Length and Track Capacities

2.3.1 Standard Car Length

The Standard Car Length for measuring approximate capacities of sidings, spurs, stub and yard tracks on the CH&FR is the 50-foot boxcar.

A 50-foot Boxcar in N scale is 3-3/4 inches.

2.3.2 Track Capacities (Definition)

Track capacities shall be specified in (scale) feet and standard car lengths.

2.4 Position and Operation of Switches

2.4.1 Normal Position

The “normal” position of all switches shall be “Lined for Main”. All switches will be returned to their normal position after each train has passed, unless the Dispatcher, Train Master, Train Orders or Track Warrant provides specific instructions otherwise.

The "normal" position of switches is indicated by a green marker, and the "reverse" or "thrown" position is indicated by a red marker.

Operation of Switches under remote Dispatcher Control are the responsibility of the Dispatcher. Train Crews shall not manually or remotely change switch settings without receiving prior permission from the Dispatcher. This permission may be granted as part of a Train Order or Track Warrant.

2.4.2 Calling Switch Positions

The position of all switches will be called by the Conductor at least 4 (four) car lengths prior to the switch and confirmed by the Engineer.

2.4.3 Clearance of Switches

Clearance points for switches are indicated by yellow markers. No train equipment shall be placed closer to the switch frog than the clearance point marker, except briefly as part of an ongoing switching move.

3. Train Movements

3.1 General

No train shall occupy the Main Line without appropriate authorization, by Timetable, Train Order, Track Warrant, or explicit directive by the Train Master. Operations within Yard Limits may be authorized independently by the designated Yard Master. Operations on branch line running tracks may be authorized by Train Order or Track Warrant.

3.2 Yard Limits

Yard limits extend from <insert loctation> to <insert location> at Frost River Yard.

Yard limits extend from Crossover 1 (MP 59) to Crossover 5 (MP 61) at Nolan Yard.

Trains entering or exiting the yard shall proceed at Restricted speed until the entire train has cleared the yard limits. Trains may not exit Yard Limits without a clearance and a valid Track Warrant from the Dispatcher. Yard Crews must obtain clearance from the Dispatcher before using the main line for switching operations. Main line trains must follow their Track Warrant when passing through Yard Limits on the main.

3.3 Passenger Stations

Passenger stations are listed in the individual subdivision sections.

Trains will exercise caution when passing passenger stations.

3.4 Whistle / Horn and Bell Instructions

Note: Non-sound-equipped trains are exempt from audible signaling requirements.

3.4.2 Bell use

The bell shall be rung under the following conditions:

  • When starting from a stop, until the train is fully in motion
  • At all grade crossings, especially in Level One Quiet Zones and Times
  • When approaching or departing a Passenger Station
  • Any time nearby people should be alerted to a train in motion (See GOR#1)

3.4.3 Quiet Zones and Times

Train Crews shall observe Quiet Zones and Times as listed below or indicated in Bulletins, Train Orders, Track Warrants or other Dispatcher instructions.

Master sound volume should be controlled by the Train Master or his designee only.

At all times, GOR#1 takes precedence, and Train Crews shall use the whistle/horn and bell as necessary to ensure the safety of personnel or civilians, regardless of established Quiet Zones or Times.

Levels of Quiet Operation are defined as follows:

  • Level One: Bell only at crossings unless vehicles or pedestrians are approaching
  • Level Two: Bell only
  • Level Three: No sound effects unless headphones are in use.

3.5 Speed Restrictions

3.5.1 General Speed Limits

Unless otherwise posted, all freight operations are limited to 50 MPH and Passenger operations are limited to 79 MPH on CH&FR tracks.

All speed limits are MAXIMUM speeds. Trains should not proceed faster than prevailing weather and track conditions permit, at the Engineer's best judgement, in keeping with General Operating Rule #1.

Crews must also observe speed restrictions imposed by the equipment and rolling stock being used on their specific train.

In any case where two applicable speed limits conflict (e.g. a signal limit is lower than the speed limit indicated in a Track Warrant), the lowest applicable speed limit shall be followed.

3.5.2 Passenger Stations

Speed limit at passenger station locations is 20mph.  Engineers should exercise caution, watching for dangerous conditions with passengers and station crew in the vicinity.

3.5.3 Speed Restrictions by Signal Aspect

In Signaled territory, speed limits shall be as follows:

Specific rules on Signal Aspects:

  • On RESTRICTED aspect, the train must be prepared to stop short of any track obstructions.
  • On STOP aspect, the train must come to a full stop before reaching the signal. After coming to a full stop, the train may proceed at Restricted speed, prepared to stop for any track obstructions.
  • On ABSOLUTE STOP, the train must come to a full stop, and may not proceed without explicit authorization from the Dispatcher or Train Master. In CTC territory, a change of CTC signal to a permissive aspect constitutes authorization to proceed at the signal-indicated speed.

3.6 Historic Equipment

The CH&FR operates a great deal of equipment designated as Historic, but still placed in general revenue service. When designated Historic equipment is in use, or Historic rolling stock is part of a consist, Train Crews should check with the Dispatcher for special handling and operating instructions. Examples of such instructions may include (but are not limited to):

  • Operating speed limits
  • Maximum coupling speeds
  • Position restrictions within the train
  • Special inspection, repair and maintenance procedures

Any such special handling instructions must be strictly followed. Crews may be held personally financially liable for violating such instructions, unless GOR#1 applies to the incident.
